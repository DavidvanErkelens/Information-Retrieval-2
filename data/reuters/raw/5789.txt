INDIA PREPARES TO LAUNCH INTERCONTINENTAL ROCKET BANGALORE India March India is preparing to launch its first intercontinental range rocket which it insists is part of a purely peaceful space program But a space department official who did not wish to be identified said the Augmented Space Launch Vehicle ASLV with the range of an intercontinental rocket and able to carry a kg satellite could be converted into a weapon after modifications Space Commission Chairman Udipi Ramachandra Rao said the ASLV will be launched between March and April depending on weather and other factors This launch will pave the way for the most important stage by the end of when we test the Polar Space Launch Vehicle PSLV Rao told Reuters The space department official said the PSLV would be able to launch kg satellites into polar orbit Rao said a successful ASLV launch could speed up the space program slightly but was not likely to solve the problem of a backup satellite for India s Insat B Insat B handles telephone communications controls of the country s television stations and provides meteorological data for farmers It is a great worry that we do not have a second satellite to back up Insat B in case it fails Even with Insat C s launch by Ariane in the first quarter of we will still be in trouble because the life of Insat B ends in Rao said He said India plans to send up Insat D as soon as possible as a backup for Insat C but will have to wait for a place on a NASA or Ariane launch vehicle We can t use Soviet vehicles because the satellites are made in the United States he said REUTER
