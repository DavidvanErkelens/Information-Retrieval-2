SURALCO ALUMINA EXPORTS DROPPED PCT IN FEB PARAMARIBO March The Surinam Aluminum Company SURALCO registered a pct drop in alumina exports in February after its operations were shut down by worker violence and guerrilla sabotage the official Surinam News Agency SNA reported SNA said Suralco s alumina exports dropped to tonnes in February from tonnes in January Aluminum exports meanwhile decreased nine pct in the same period to tonnes from tonnes Suralco s alumina refinery at Paranam miles south of the capital was shut down February after workers destroyed plant and computer equipment in a protest over layoffs at the company s nearby aluminum smelter The smelter was shut down January after anti government guerrillas dynamited two electricity towers which transmit power to the plant from the Afobaka dam The alumina refinery owned jointly by Suralco and Billiton NV a Royal Dutch Shell subsidiary was reopened March But the smelter remains closed as do the Suralco mines at Moengo miles east of Paramaribo which were closed down by guerrillas last November Reuter
