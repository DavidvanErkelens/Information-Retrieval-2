AIRBUS SIGNS ACCORD ON CFM ENGINE FOR A PLANE PARIS April The European Airbus Industrie consortium today formally signed an agreement with the Franco U S Group CFM International for an improved version of the CFM engine to power the A and A aircraft an Airbus statement said Airbus said yesterday that it chosen the CFM S engine to be produced by General Electric of the U S And SNECMA of France because the International Aero Engine IAE high technology superfan engine would not be ready by early when the A plane is due to become operational The five nation IAE consortium has decided not to go ahead with the proposed launch of the superfan a spokesman for consortium member Rolls Royce Plc said today adding that the project had not been cancelled and could be offered later on Airbus said the CFM S will have a thrust of lbs and an improved fuel burn of four per cent compared with the CFM A which is now being operated on the A flight test aircraft It will be available for deliveries of the A in May At the same time the Airbus statement said Airbus Industrie and General Electric have concluded an accord for an improved version of the GE CF C engine with lbs of thrust and a three per cent improvement in specific fuel consumption to power the A aircraft which will be introduced in spring This continues the long standing history of all Airbus launches being made with either GE or CFMI engines Airbus Industrie president Jean Pierson said in the statement Airbus Industrie has already worked with CFMI to launch one new aircraft programme the A in early and is happy to build on this relationship by launching the A with an improved version of the same engine Pierson said Airbus Industrie said it has so far received a total of commitments for the A and A from nine customers Reuter
