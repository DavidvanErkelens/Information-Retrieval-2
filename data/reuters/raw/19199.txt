DUTCH TO MEET PUBLIC SECTOR DEFICIT TARGET THE HAGUE June Dutch public authorities are on course to meet the public sector deficit target of pct of net national income NNI despite excess spending by the central government according to a leaked official report The so called Spring Report giving latest revenue and expenditure figures and to be discussed by the Dutch cabinet today was leaked in advance to the Dutch press The Dutch Finance Ministry declined to comment on the press reports Newspapers quoted the report drafted by Finance Minister Onno Ruding as saying unexpected rises in revenue would compensate for excess spending of billion guilders Ruding putting this year s public sector borrowing requirement percentage point below last September s target at pct criticised the excess spending but will not seek additional spending cuts for because of the surprise boost in revenue the report was quoted as saying Excess spending by the central government was put at billion guilders while market factors accounted for another mln in unbudgetted expenditure But revenue was boosted by an unexpected billion including billion in tax revenue and mln guilders in income from natural gas sales Ruding has said the excess spending will burden the budget with an additional billion and the cabinet has already pledged billion in cuts to narrow the gap The cabinet is to discuss in coming months what further austerity measures will be needed next year to keep the government on target for its aim of a public sector borrowing requirement of no more than pct of NNI Ruding has already met opposition from the Liberal and Christian Democrat factions carrying the government majority in parliament to his calls for more spending cuts in the budget to counter a slow down in economic growth REUTER
