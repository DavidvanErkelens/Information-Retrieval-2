CANADA SETS WIDE RANGING PERSONAL TAX CHANGES OTTAWA June Finance Minister Michael Wilson unveiled a wide ranging reform of the personal tax system that includes limiting the capital gains exemption and a sharp cut in the dividend tax credit With most changes effective at the first of next year Wilson also announced he was cutting the number of tax brackets from to three He said the changes will cut personal tax revenues by two billion dlrs in and by more than billion dlrs over the next five years Most Canadians will pay lower taxes because of two far reaching changes A new structure of federal income tax rates and the conversion of exemptions and deductions to tax credits Wilson told the House of Commons The new tax brackets will be pct on the first dlrs of taxable income pct on the next dlrs and pct on taxable income in excess of dlrs The maximum tax rate is pct under the current system In a major reversal of his own initiative Wilson said the controversial dlrs capital gains exemption will be reduced to dlrs over an investors lifetime Wilson introduced the exemption shortly after taking office in as a a way of stimulating investment but it was sharply criticized by the opposition as over generous to wealthy investors The dlr lifetime exemption will be kept on the sale of farm land and for small businesess however Also the taxable portion of a capital gain will increase from pct currently to pct in and pct in The dividend tax credit will be reduced from pct to pct and the deduction for up to dlrs of interest and dividend income will be eliminated in Wilson said tax treatment for registered retirement savings plan contributions will be maintained but the phase in of the increase in the maximum limit to dlrs will be delayed four years to Reuter
