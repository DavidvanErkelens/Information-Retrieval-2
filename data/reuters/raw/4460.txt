PAINEWEBBER PWJ BROKER INDICTED IN SCHEME NEW YORK March A former broker at PaineWebber Inc was charged by a federal grand jury with involvement in a dlr money laundering scheme according to indictment papers Gary Eder a former vice president at PaineWebber was charged with conspiracy and falsifying brokerage records in the two count indictment According to the indictment Eder produced more brokerage commissions than any other broker at PaineWebber and was its most highly paid broker between Eder is charged with conspiring with unnamed supervisors at PaineWebber to prevent the filing of Currency Transaction Reports with the Internal Revenue Service Federal law requires that reports be filed with the IRS for any single cash transaction in one day of more than dlrs Asked about the charges a Painewebber spokeswoman said the company had not seen the indictment and had no comment She said Eder was suspended January and resigned from the firm on february David Spears the federal prosecutor in charge of the case said Eder received cash from individual customers in amounts ranging up to dlrs at a time but avoided filing reports by depositing the cash into the customers accounts in amounts just under dlrs on several different days If convicted Eder could receive a maximum jail term of ten years and dlrs in fines Reuter
