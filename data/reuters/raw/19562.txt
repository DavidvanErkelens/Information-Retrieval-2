FRENCH CHANNEL TF PRESIDENT SEES PROFIT IN PARIS June France s state run television channel TF which is in the process of being privatised should achieve a profit of around francs per share next year after several years of losses but will not make a profit this year the new TF president Francis Bouygues said Interviewed on TF on the eve of the public flotation of pct of the channel s equity Bouygues said in we have to make a profit and we will make a profit I envisage per share profits of francs he said I am very optimistic Bouygues said exceptional costs associated with the restructuring of the station would prevent a profit this year The state sold a pct stake in TF in April to a consortium led by Bouygues a construction magnate and including British press baron Robert Maxwell Tomorrow pct of the shares go on sale to the public at francs each while pct will be offered at a special price of francs a share to the station s staff Bouygues said a dividend would be paid to shareholders next year if the station did return to profit Asked if he thought the flotation price was right Bouygues said I paid virtually double I consider that it is a good buy The Bouygues consortium paid three billion francs for its pct controlling stake in April After the two week public flotation TF shares will start trading on the second market here in late July REUTER
