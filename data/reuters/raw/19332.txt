BROOKLYN UNION SAYS A RATE HIKE WOULD BE MODEST NEW YORK June Brooklyn Union Gas BU a New York gas utility company said it will try to limit any rate hikes to customers by arranging alternative supplies to replace the spot natural gas that Transco Energy Co E no longer delivers a company official told Reuters Edward Sondey vice president of supply said that under a grandfather clause Brooklyn Union could request Transco to transmit mln cubic feet mcf a day of spot natural gas after purchasing mcf of contract gas from Transco Brooklyn Union has been taking only pct or mcf of the contract gas from Transco and acquiring about mcf on the spot or non contract market he said Lacking access to spot gas through Transco would not cost Brooklyn Union more than cts per mln btu for now Sondey said as Transco contract gas costs dlrs per mln compared to about two dlrs for spot gas The rate increase will not be serious Sondey said The contract price at dlrs per mln BTU has been effective since May after Transco lowered its commodity rate to reflect seasonal weakness making it the lowest in the months ended May according to report submitted by Brooklyn Union to New York Public Utility Commission In peak demand season Transco s contract gas reached as high as to dlrs per mln btu a commission official said Industry analysts expected little adverse effect from interrupted spot supplies on Brooklyn Union s earnings for its current fiscal year ending September But if the open access problem to Transco is not resolved this winter high cost of contract gas could cut into the company s profit they said Reuter
