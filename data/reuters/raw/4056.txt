AWB SAYS AUSTRALIAN WHEAT SALES OVER MLN TONNES MELBOURNE March The Australian Wheat Board s AWB export program is well advanced with over mln tonnes already sold AWB general manager Ron Paice said We are certainly within reach of our mln tonne export target for the year he said in a statement He did not detail the commitments already made but an AWB spokesman said they include sales to Egypt China Iran the Soviet Union and Iraq In the wheat year ended September the AWB exported a record mln tonnes Paice also said the Australian wheat harvest has ended after a long cool summer with mln tonnes delivered to the AWB The season produced another good crop with only pct of receivals being downgraded to feed quality he said However it is likely that some weather damaged grain was still being held on farms and further milling and feed wheat may be delivered following the recent announcement of the final Guaranteed Minimum Price for he said Paice did not give a crop estimate but the AWB s February Wheat Australia publication put the crop at mln tonnes But the AWB spokesman said it is likely this estimate could turn out to be too high based on the receivals level and the final crop figure would probably be nearer to mln tonnes The official estimate is not yet available In the season the AWB received mln tonnes of the mln tonne crop Another tonnes was retained on farm and sold under the permit system introduced in to allow farmers to sell feed wheat to the grain trade outside the AWB s receival system according to Bureau of Agricultural Economics data REUTER
