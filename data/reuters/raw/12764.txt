S KOREA ATTEMPTS TO COOL OVER HEATED STOCK MARKET SEOUL April South Korea will press institutional investors to sell shares they are holding and oblige them to buy monetary stabilisation bonds if they want to make further stock investments a finance ministry spokesman said He said Finance Minister Chung In yong Bank of Korea Governor Park Sung sang and officials of the Securities Supervisory Board agreed that the stock market was over heated and decided to take measures to cool it down Ministry officials said the recent market boom was fuelled mainly by ample liquidity and excessive demand from institutions and speculative investors The spokesman said industrial firms with bank loans worth billion won or more should either issue convertible bonds or offer new shares in order to raise funds to help repay their loans Securities houses will not be allowed to hold shares worth more than pct of their paid in capital and investment trust firms pct the spokesman said But he did not give a deadline for compliance Other insitutitions should also reduce the volume of their share holdings he added The composite index a weighted average of listed firms closed at yesterday It started at The spokesman said industrial firms with bank loans worth billion won or more should either issue convertible bonds or offer new shares in order to raise funds to help repay their loans Securities houses will not be allowed to hold shares worth more than pct of their paid in capital and investment trust firms pct the spokesman said But he did not give a deadline for compliance Other insitutitions should also reduce the volume of their share holdings he added The composite index a weighted average of listed firms closed at yesterday It started at REUTER
