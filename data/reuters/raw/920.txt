COMALCO SAYS LOWER COSTS HELPED RETURN TO PROFITS MELBOURNE March Comalco Ltd CMAC S said its return to profit reflected reduced costs improved primary aluminium prices and its withdrawal from a Japanese smelter venture It said the earlier reported mln dlr profit for the year ended December against a mln dlr loss in was also aided by lower interest rates on U S Dollar debt and greater sales of bauxite and aluminium Comalco said it expected to pay at least a four cents per share final dividend delayed until July to take advantage of proposed dividend imputation laws This would make five cents for the year against a first and final of one cent in Comalco said the aluminium industry continued to suffer from low prices and excess capacity though the weak Australian dollar had helped earnings Withdrawal from the Showa Aluminium Industries KK joint venture had been recapitalised in expansion by the New Zealand Aluminium Smelters Ltd project with Japan s Sumitomo Aluminium Smelting Co Ltd permitting repayments and increases in liquid funds totalling mln dlrs it said As previously reported Comalco s Commonwealth Aluminium Corp unit has conditionally agreed to sell its smelter at Goldendale Washington and port facilities at Portland Oregon to Columbia Aluminium Corp Comalco said it had made a mln dlr extraordinary provision for Goldendale losses and closure costs but that if the sales agreement were completed it would reduce the provision made in the accounts The other items in the total extraordinary loss of mln dlrs were a mln write off of unrealised exchange losses and mln for an increase in future tax provision REUTER
