FINAL CORN CERTS SEEN AT BILLION DLRS WASHINGTON March The Agriculture Department will have to release an estimated billion dlrs worth of in kind certificates or certs to corn farmers this fall if USDA decides to maintain an equal split of total feedgrain deficiency payments in cash and certs USDA officials said Final crop deficiency payments will be available to feed grain producers in October Wheat deficiency payments for the crop now complete were made in a cash certificate split and USDA officials have indicated they want to do the same for feedgrains Over billion dlrs of an estimated billion in corn deficiency payments have already been announced with billion dlrs pct set in certificates The final payment must be billion dlrs in certs pct for a split However the release this month of almost mln dlrs of deficiency payments was equally divided in cash and certs doing nothing to bring up the percentage of certs Some USDA analysts said the payments reflected hesitation from the Office of Managament and Budget to allow another huge release of certs onto the market Bills are pending in Congress to move up the final feedgrain deficiency payment to as soon as five months after harvest as possible rather than the current year lag time Neither Rep Edward Madigan R Ill nor Sen Bob Dole R Kans sponsors of the bills have brought the legislation forward Aides said timing will depend on budget discussions Reuter
