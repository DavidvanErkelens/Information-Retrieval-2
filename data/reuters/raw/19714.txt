BRAZIL MOTOR INDUSTRY SENDS ON LEAVE SAO PAULO June Brazil s motor industry facing the worst crisis of its history today sent thousands of workers on collective leave because of a sharp drop in demand Company spokesmen said Autolatina which last Friday laid off workers sent nearly of its employees in Brazil on collective leave until July General Motors do Brasil said it would give days leave to part of its workforce from Wednesday Fiat is giving workers a month s holiday from July Jacy Mendonca industrial relations director of Autolatina a merger of Volkswagen and Ford in Brazil and Argentina said last week this would be the worst year ever for Brazil s auto industry He forecast that would be worse than when domestic sales dropped pct to units This year we ll be lucky if we can sell units he said Since Friday there have been two positive developments for the industry The government announced that it was scrapping a pct surcharge on all new car sales The surcharge created in July last year was originally set at pct to help curb a car buying boom and was in theory refundable Brazilian car distributors which since June had halted all car purchases in protest against high government taxes responded to the government move by resuming purchases But industry spokesmen said taxes on vehicles were still high pct and the sector still faced major problems Reuter
