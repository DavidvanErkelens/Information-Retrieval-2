TANZANIA RAISES FUEL PRICES TAXES IN BUDGET DAR ES SALAAM June The Tanzanian government in its second annual budget since embarking on an economic recovery program inspired by the International Monetary Fund announced increases in fuel prices and sales tax and higher levies on government services Finance Minister Cleopa Msuya said the government expected to spend billion shillings in the financial year pct more than this year s estimate Revenue would provide billion shillings leaving a deficit of billion to be financed through domestic and foreign loans This year s budget initially projected a deficit of only billion shillings but in mid year the government had to impose new taxes to keep the deficit roughly on target No figure for the final deficit was avaialble The budget gave civil servants a pct pay rise with effect from July to compensate for inflation of more than pct Msuya said regular petrol would go up pct with similar increases in the cost of premium and kerosene Diesel would rise by pct The higher levies affect official transactions such as road tolls vehicle transfers and various licence fees he added REUTER
