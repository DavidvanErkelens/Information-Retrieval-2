USDA REPORTS SEEN NEGATIVE TO LIVESTOCK FUTURES By Jerry Bieszk Reuters CHICAGO April Commission house livestock analysts agreed with Chicago Mercantile Exchange floor traders in calling today s USDA state quarterly cattle on feed report and cold storage report for pork bellies negative June live cattle futures are called to cent lower on Monday and back months of cattle are expected to cent lower Pork bellies are expected to cent or more lower the analysts said Disappointment was voiced over the placement and total on feed figures in the cattle report Both the quarterly section and monthly state part showed the amount placed on feed and on feed numbers at the high end of expectations The other disappearance figure of only three pct above a year ago in the state section was also vieved as negative Most of the early guesses predicted a much larger number for death loss following the two winter snow storms that struck the west in late March they said However weight groupings were friendly to the nearby futures and should prompt some bull spreading on the decline they said William Arndt from Dean Witter noted that the to lb steers and to lb heifers were at pct of a year ago and should lend some support to June futures However to lb steers at pct will weigh on August he said The big reduction in cattle weighing over lbs should be friendly to the market for at least the next to days AGE Clearing analyst Jerry Abbenhaus said Movement of bellies into frozen storage was at the high end of expectations and should weigh on futures tomorrow especially as futures prices ended weak today they said Other parts of the cold storage report were also viewed as negative to livestock and meat futures We have a lot of poultry in storage Even though we have smaller pork supplies the decline is not enough to offset the increases in poultry Shearson Lehman analyst Chuck Levitt said Also there is more beef in storage than last year and this was achieved on smaller production Reuter
