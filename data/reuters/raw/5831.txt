SINGAPORE ENDS SCHEME TO HELP BROKING FIRMS SINGAPORE March The Stock Exchange of Singapore has decided to discontinue a mln dlr standby credit facility for broking firms facing financial problems because of the failure of Pan Electric Industries Ltd an exchange statement said The credit line from four local banks was set up on December to bail out firms involved in forward contracts with Pan Electric which went into receivership in November owing mln dlrs The company was wound up last year Local broking houses have been contributing a pct levy on share transactions to the scheme but will no longer do so the statement said REUTER
