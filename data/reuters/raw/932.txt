AIRBUS SIGNS ONE BILLION DLR JAPANESE CONTRACT PARIS March The European Airbus Industrie consortium has signed a one billion dlr contract to sell A short haul jets to All Nippon Airways of Japan with an option on the sale of a further planes an Airbus spokesman said today The planes on firm order will be delivered between September and November and will be powered either by CFM S engines made by the Franco U S CFM International consortium or V S engines built by the International Aero Engine group The spokesman declined to comment on a New York Times report that Airbus is about to announce a sale to American Airlines But industry sources said American was about to announce the purchase of wide bodied planes from Airbus as well as s from Boeing Co BAN Airbus has already signed large deals with Pan Am Corp PNN NWA Inc NWA and Eastern Airlines Inc EALN as well as a smaller contract with Continental Airlines The A made its first flight on February and is not due to enter commercial service until March next year The All Nippon purchase brings the total number of commitments to the A to from airlines comprising firm orders and options REUTER
